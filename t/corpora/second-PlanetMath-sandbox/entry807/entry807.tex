For $p,q \in \reals$, $1<p,q<\infty$ we say $p$ and $q$ are \emph{\PMlinkescapetext{conjugate indices}} if $\frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} = 1$.  Formally, we will also define $q = \infty$ as conjugate to $p=1$ and vice versa.

Conjugate indices are used in the \PMlinkname{H\"older inequality}{HolderInequality} and more generally to define conjugate spaces.
